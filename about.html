About KTHQ

Why This Exists

I started KTHQ because I kept solving the same problems over and over and watching other contracting officers do the same. The good guidance was buried in SharePoint sites behind CAC readers, scattered across outdated PDFs, or locked in the heads of people who retired three years ago. New COs were getting thrown into the deep end with a warrant and a "good luck." I wanted to build the resource I wish I had when I was starting out.

Who I Am

I'm an active federal contracting professional. Before contracting, I worked as an Inspector General inspector, which gave me a front-row seat to what happens when the process breaks down and nobody can explain why they did what they did. That experience shapes everything on this site: every tool, every training walkthrough, every piece of guidance is built around doing the work correctly and being able to defend your decisions.

I also co-run the Contracting Memes Center of Excellence (CMCoE) on Facebook, because sometimes you just need to laugh about a FAR Part 15 evaluation that went sideways. If you know, you know.

What You'll Find Here

KTHQ is built for contracting officers at every level. If you just got your warrant and need to figure out how to put a solicitation together, the training walkthroughs will take you step by step. If you're a deployed CCO writing manual contracts in a tent, the contingency tools are built specifically for you. If you've been doing this for years and just want a fast reference, the FAR overhaul tracker, GAO decision feed, and links page are designed to get you answers without the noise.

Everything here is free. No paywall, no login, no ads. It will stay that way. If KTHQ saves you time or helps you get through a tough action, that's the whole point.

Get Involved

If you have ideas for new tools, spot an error, or want to contribute training content, reach out through the Contact page. This site gets better when practitioners share what works in the field. You can also support the project on Buy Me a Coffee if you want to help keep the lights on.
